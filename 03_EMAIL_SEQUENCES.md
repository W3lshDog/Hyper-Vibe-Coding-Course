### EMAIL 6: Day 3 - What Makes Great Design

**Subject Line:** [Day 3/7] The secret to apps that feel ALIVE ✨

**From:** You

**Preview:** It's not what you think...

---

**BODY:**

Quick lesson before today's challenge:

**Why do some apps feel alive and others feel dead?**

It's not fancy animations. It's not perfect code. It's not even that complicated.

It's **taste.**

Taste means:
- Choosing 3-4 colors that work together (not 6 random ones)
- Using fonts that tell a story (not generic defaults)
- Adding personality to every interaction (not robotic responses)
- Keeping things minimal, not cluttered

---

**Here's the formula:**

**Colors:** Pick 1 dominant + 1 secondary + 1 accent
- Dominant (60% of the app): Your main color
- Secondary (30%): Complements the main
- Accent (10%): Highlights, buttons, CTAs

**Fonts:** Pick 2 total
- Display font: Big, bold, distinctive (titles)
- Body font: Clean, readable (text)

(Use Google Fonts, it's free and has 1000+ options)

**Spacing:** Use consistent gaps
- 8px between small elements
- 16px between sections
- 32px between major sections

---

Think about an app YOU would actually use.

### EMAIL 8: Day 5 - The Prompt Engineering Masterclass

**Subject Line:** [Day 5/7] The 3 secrets professional prompters use 🧙

**From:** You

**Preview:** Transform your results with these techniques...

---

**BODY:**

Most people prompt wrong.

They say things like: "Build a weather app"

Generic result? Generic weather app. Boring.

**Here's what the pros do:**

---

**SECRET #1: GIVE AI A ROLE**

"You're an engineer at a startup..." → gives AI your vibe
"You're a designer who loves bold colors..." → sets tone
"You're building for ADHD users..." → context for decisions

**SECRET #2: DESCRIBE THE FEELING, NOT THE APPEARANCE**

Bad: "Use blue and white"
Good: "Make it feel calm and trustworthy. Use soft colors that don't overload"

Bad: "Add animations"
Good: "Add satisfying button interactions. When they click, celebrate them with confetti"

Bad: "Make it professional"
Good: "Make it feel like a premium app, not a template"

**SECRET #3: GIVE PERMISSION TO BE CREATIVE**

"Keep it simple" → AI defaults to boring
"Surprise me with creative touches" → AI explores possibilities
"Make it feel alive, not corporate" → AI goes wild in good ways

---

**THE FORMULA:**

```
[ROLE]: "You're a designer who builds..."
[CONTEXT]: "For users who need... this app does..."
[FEATURES]: "Include: 1, 2, 3..."
[TASTE]: "Make it feel [adjective]. Use [colors]. Add [touches]..."
[PERMISSION]: "Surprise me. Make it memorable."
```
